Registration

Instructions:

  • Form a team of one to five members. Faculty can be added (optional).
  • Submit your project idea and present a ppt for evaluation in the semifinalist selection process.
  • The prototype of your project will undergo evaluation for the finalist selection.
  • Certificates will be awarded to both semifinalists and finalists of the event.
  • The top three finalists will receive cash prizes as recognition for their outstanding contributions.

Guidelines:

  • We are looking forward to seeing innovative ideas & solutions based on the following points.
  • You are required to submit your solution idea in the form of a PowerPoint Presentation (or PDF) with the following mandate points which are also very important when you think of your ideas.
  • Problem statement (What exact problem do you want to solve with your ideas and solutions?) Ideas & solutions to solve the problem (How would you utilize technology?)\ Uniqueness & feasibility (Even if you have a good idea, there is no meaning if it is impossible to deploy. Also, there is no meaning to create copy ideas/solutions that already exist)
  • How much impact will be provided to society by your solution?.
  • We will check how much you clearly define the problem statement and how you try to approach your ideas & solutions. If you can also include technology info in your presentation, it will be helpful for us to understand your solution more..

Alignment with Verticals:

All projects and solutions must align with the chosen vertical and address challenges or opportunities within that domain.

Definition of Scope:

Establish clear objectives and boundaries within the chosen vertical. Teams should define the specific problem and they aim to solve, ensuring alignment with the vertical. Teams should stay focused, maintain feasibility, and communicate their project's goals effectively.

Originality:

Solutions presented should be original and developed during the ideathon, avoiding plagiarism or copyright infringement.

Ethical Considerations:

Participants should consider ethical implications related to the vertical, including privacy, sustainability, and social responsibility.

Problem Solving:

Solutions should aim to solve real-world problems or contribute positively to the chosen vertical.
